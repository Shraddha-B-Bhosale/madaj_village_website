Ramai Awas Yojana (Housing Scheme)

About the Scheme: Ramai Awas Yojana is a housing scheme started by the Government of Maharashtra. Under this scheme, permanent (pucca) houses are given to poor families who do not have their own house.

Eligibility: Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Nav Bauddha (Neo-Buddhist) families.

Conditions:

  • Family must not already own a pucca house.
  • Name should be in BPL or SECC list.
  • Land should be available for construction.
  • Priority to widows, disabled persons, women-headed families.
  • Financial help is given in installments.

Benefit: A free pucca house is provided to eligible poor families.

Phalbag Yojana (Fruit Orchard Scheme)

About the Scheme: A scheme by the Government of Maharashtra to promote fruit orchards like mango, pomegranate, guava, orange, custard apple, etc.

Eligibility (Caste/Category): All farmers can apply. Special priority for SC, ST, small/marginal farmers, and women farmers.

Conditions:

  • Farmer must have suitable land for fruit orchard.
  • Minimum and maximum land area limit as per crop (0.25 to 4 hectare approx).
  • Orchard must be maintained for 3 years.
  • Subsidy given in installments depending on crop.
  • Approved saplings/seedlings must be used from registered nurseries.

Benefit: Subsidy on fruit plantation cost + technical guidance. Helps farmers earn more in future.

Sinchan Viheer Yojana (Irrigation Well Scheme)

About the Scheme: A government scheme to provide financial help to farmers for constructing irrigation wells on their land to ensure water supply for crops.

Eligibility (Caste/Category): All farmers can apply. Priority for SC, ST, small and marginal farmers, and women farmers.

Conditions:

  • Farmer must own the land where the well will be built.
  • Land records should be in farmer‚Äôs name.
  • Subsidy is only for new wells, not for repairing old ones.
  • Well should be used for irrigation purpose only.
  • Farmer has to contribute some part of the cost.
  • Work should follow technical guidelines of agriculture/irrigation department.

Benefit: Subsidy for irrigation wells, more water availability for farming, better crop yield, and more income for farmers.

Janavaracha Goth Yojana (Cattle Shed Scheme)

About the Scheme: A government scheme to support poor farmers and cattle owners by providing financial help to build a cattle shed (goth) for animals like cows, buffaloes, goats, etc.

Eligibility (Caste/Category): All farmers and cattle owners can apply. Priority for SC, ST, small/marginal farmers, and women beneficiaries.

Conditions:

  • Applicant must own cattle and land/space for the shed.
  • The shed should be used only for keeping animals.
  • Farmer has to share some part of the cost; rest is subsidy.
  • Construction must follow government-approved size/design.
  • Priority to poor families, widows, disabled persons, and women-headed families.

Benefit: Subsidy for building cattle shed, safe shelter for animals, better milk production, and improved income for farmers.

Shoshkhadda Yojana (Soak Pit Scheme)

About the Scheme: A government scheme to construct soak pits in villages. It helps manage wastewater, keeps surroundings clean, and improves sanitation.

Eligibility (Caste/Category): All rural households can apply. Priority for SC, ST, BPL families, and Swachh Bharat Mission villages.

Conditions:

  • Household must not already have a proper wastewater system.
  • Space/land should be available near the house.
  • Soak pit must be built as per design of Rural Development Department.
  • Family/Gram Panchayat should maintain it after construction.
  • Priority to poor, landless, and women-headed households.

Benefit: Financial help for soak pit construction, reduces wastewater problems, prevents mosquito breeding, and keeps village clean.

Jaltara Yojana (Water Conservation Scheme)

About the Scheme: A government scheme in Maharashtra for water conservation and groundwater recharge. Structures like farm ponds, compartment bunds, trenches, and recharge pits are built to store rainwater.

Eligibility (Caste/Category): All farmers can apply. Priority for small/marginal farmers, SC/ST farmers, and women farmers.

Conditions:

  • Applicant must own agricultural land.
  • Land must be suitable for water conservation work.
  • Beneficiary must maintain the structure after construction.
  • One farmer/family can take benefit only once.
  • Selection is done by Gram Panchayat/Water Conservation Department.

Benefit: Free or subsidized support for water conservation structures, improves irrigation, increases groundwater, and helps farming throughout the year.

Majhi Vasundhara Abhiyan (My Earth Campaign)

About the Scheme: An environmental program by the Maharashtra Government to protect nature and fight climate change through tree plantation, clean water, clean air, waste management, and renewable energy.

Eligibility: All Gram Panchayats, Municipal Councils, Municipal Corporations, and Nagar Panchayats in Maharashtra. Any caste/religion people can join. Priority for villages/cities with strong public participation.

Main Components (5 Elements):

  • Air (Vayu): Reduce pollution, use clean energy.
  • Water (Jal): Save rivers, lakes, wells, do rainwater harvesting.
  • Earth (Bhoomi): Tree plantation, increase greenery.
  • Fire (Agni): Use solar, biogas, other renewable energy.
  • Sky (Aakash): Eco-friendly festivals, awareness programs.

Conditions:

  • Local body must prepare and follow action plan.
  • Public participation is compulsory.
  • Progress report should be submitted through app/website.
  • Awards given to best performing villages/cities.

Benefits: Cleaner environment, more greenery, support for eco-friendly projects, awards and recognition.

Nagari Suvidha Yojana (Urban Civic Amenities Scheme)

About the Scheme: A scheme by the Maharashtra Government to provide basic civic facilities like water, roads, drainage, sanitation, and streetlights in towns and cities.

Eligibility: Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats, and Municipal Corporations. All urban citizens get benefits. No caste restriction.

Facilities Covered:

  • Drinking water supply.
  • Drainage and sanitation system.
  • Roads and footpaths.
  • Streetlights and electricity connections.
  • Parks, gardens, community halls, public toilets.
  • Solid waste management.

Conditions:

  • Project must be approved by local body.
  • Funds only for public benefit, not private use.
  • Government monitoring is compulsory.
  • Priority for areas with poor facilities.

Benefits: Better living standards, clean water, sanitation, safe roads, and healthy environment in cities and towns.

Shet Raste / Panand Raste Yojana (Farm & Approach Roads Scheme)

About the Scheme: A Maharashtra Government scheme to construct and repair approach roads and farm roads in villages so that farmers can easily reach their farms and markets.

Eligibility: All farmers and villagers. No caste restriction. Priority for areas without proper roads.

Facilities Covered:

  • New farm approach road construction.
  • Repair of damaged farm roads.
  • Gravel/tar roads for easy transport of crops.
  • Roads connecting farms to main roads or markets.

Conditions:

  • Land should be available for road construction.
  • Road must be for public/farm use, not private use.
  • Work under Gram Panchayat / Zilla Parishad supervision.
  • Priority for remote villages with poor connectivity.

Benefits: Easy crop transport, better village connectivity, saves time and money, and supports rural economy.

Birth Certificate

About: A birth certificate is an official document that proves the birth of a child. It is issued by the Gram Panchayat or Municipal Corporation.

Who can apply:

  • Parents of the newborn child.
  • Guardian (if parents are not available).

Required Documents:

  • Application form
  • Hospital birth proof / certificate from doctor
  • Parents Aadhaar card
  • Address proof

Process (in short):

  1. Fill the application form at Gram Panchayat / online portal.
  2. Attach required documents.
  3. Submit to Panchayat office / Municipality.
  4. Verification by official.
  5. Certificate issued.

Processing Time: Within 7 to 15 working days after document verification.

Benefit: It is a legal proof of identity, age, and nationality. Required for school admission, government schemes, and other services.
